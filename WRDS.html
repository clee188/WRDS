DEI Awareness

April 16, 2025

DEI (more commonly known as Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion) has been under attack from the Trump administration. The reason why it is so important is that it is a broad-based policy that protects vulnerable groups. Getting rid of these DEI policies means a huge underrepresentation for minorities. It means people of different ethnic backgrounds are at risk, people with disabilities are at risk, and employees in general. Studies find that workplaces with greater diversity seem to benefit their establishments. For example, hospitals with more racial diversity have shown greater patient satisfaction. Moreover, without DEI efforts, minority groups may continue to face barriers in accessing healthcare, education, and housing. Historically, these communities have experienced systemic exclusion from essential services, which DEI programs seek to address. If DEI policies are eliminated, the cycle of inequality that these groups face could become even more entrenched, leading to greater disparities in health outcomes, educational achievement, and economic mobility. In conclusion, eliminating DEI policies not only undermines progress in creating inclusive and fair environments, but it also puts at risk the well-being and advancement of vulnerable communities. By maintaining and strengthening these initiatives, society can work toward a more equitable future for all.

For more insights on the elimination of DEI programs and its effects on racial health equity, refer to the Kaiser Family Foundation issue brief on DEI and health equity.
